Third-Party Infrastructure

Fardomax uses the following third-party services solely for connection establishment. These services never see your files or encryption keys:

  • PeerJS Signal Server (0.peerjs.com) — A free, open-source WebRTC signaling server. It receives temporary peer IDs and connection metadata (SDP/ICE) for the duration of connection setup only. No files, no keys, no content ever passes through it.
  • Open Relay Project (openrelay.metered.ca) — Free TURN relay servers used when direct P2P connection cannot be established (e.g., strict NAT or corporate firewalls). All data relayed is AES-256-GCM encrypted — the relay operator sees only opaque ciphertext.
  • Metered.ca TURN Servers — Secondary TURN relay fallback. Same guarantees as above — all relayed traffic is end-to-end encrypted before it reaches the relay.
  • Google STUN Servers (stun.l.google.com) — Used only to discover your public IP/port for P2P hole-punching. No data is sent through STUN servers.
  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com) — Loads the Orbitron, Share Tech Mono, and Exo 2 fonts. Google may log the request per their own privacy policy. No user-specific data is passed.

Encryption & Security Architecture

Every file transferred through Fardomax is encrypted using AES-256-GCM (Advanced Encryption Standard, 256-bit key, Galois/Counter Mode) — the same encryption standard used by governments and militaries worldwide.

  • Key Generation — A unique 256-bit key is generated in your browser using the Web Crypto API (crypto.subtle.generateKey) for every transfer session. The key never leaves your browser except via the share URL.
  • Authenticated Encryption — AES-GCM provides both confidentiality and integrity. Any tampering with encrypted data in transit will cause decryption to fail, protecting against man-in-the-middle attacks.
  • Per-Chunk IV — Each 64 KB chunk is encrypted with a fresh random 96-bit initialization vector, preventing patterns from emerging even in identical data.
  • Transport Security — All signaling traffic uses TLS 1.3. WebRTC data channels use DTLS-SRTP by specification.

Your Rights

Because we collect no personal data, most data rights (access, deletion, portability) are automatically satisfied — there is nothing to access, delete, or port. However, your rights under applicable law include:

  • Right to be Forgotten — There is nothing stored to forget. Closing the transfer session terminates all connection data.
  • Right to Data Portability — Your files never leave your control. They exist on your device and the recipient's device only.
  • Right to Transparency — This policy and our source code are the full transparency record.
  • GDPR / CCPA Compliance — Our zero-data architecture means we do not fall under data controller obligations as we process no personal data at rest.

Children's Privacy

Fardomax does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13 (or under 16 in the EU). As we collect no personal information from any user, this protection is universal. Parents who believe their child has transmitted sensitive information via our service should note that we have no way to access or retain that information — it was transferred directly between devices.
